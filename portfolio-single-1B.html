Goal

To create an effective and satisfying user interface design for a high-level design challenge.

1. User evaluation.

2. Ideate, design, iterate.

3. Task challenge & user test.

For this class project in User Interface Design, I was tasked with solving how a UX Design student at Austin Community College can manage and navigate the onslaught of online resources available, recommended or required during their time as a student. I was also tasked with using an iterative design process to create and test interface solutions for ​the discovered needs, with the only constraint being to work within the technical limitations of existing devices and features.

The Challenge

Give a UX Design student an interface to locate, vet and read or interact with validated resources to further their learning and career. Students should be able to search for resources by topic, validate the credential or quality of the resource, and apply their learnings to their career goals.

User Evaluation

  • Who am I designing for?
  • What are their needs, behaviors and goals?
I began by evaluating the needs of my users. As a UX Design student myself, I drew on my own experience managing online resources (often articles). I realized I sometimes found articles myself, but primarily I received articles forwarded by professors.
My own behaviors involved locating and reading articles relevant to my courses or career topics; it also involved retaining those articles for future reference when completing course assignments, which I found particularly useful when developing new skills or building on my aptitude for performing user design work. My needs may have varied from simply satisfying course requirements to further educating myself outside of class.

Research and statement of finding

My online research revealed there are no reliable methods or guidepoints to direct a user towards consistent, quality UX resources. While quality articles exist, and there are even good collections at sites like Medium.com, extensive user research would be needed to form a detailed solution with vast coverage for UX students specifically. Since this challenge is structured for a quick deliverable, and because it's intended to aid ACC students in particular, I decided to focus my strategy on a process of resource vetting/approval from professors and fellow students based on school curriculum. This would not only make for easier design parameters, but would (theoretically) provide trustworthy assistance and context for users.

Ideate: Crazy 8's

I began ideating using Crazy 8's. This helped generate some layout ideas and features.
Ideas I chose to explore as a result include...
  • Applying a strong visual approach to organizing articles by course.
  • Applying a rating system.
  • Applying filter options.
  • Applying toggle options to activate/deactivate features.

Ideate: Low-fidelity sketches & Silent critique

  • Low-fidelity sketches
  • Silent critique
Next I used a desktop layout to explore concepts and relationships among different features. One concept that eventually became the focus of my user test was a suggestions feature!

Feedback from class was especially helpful. Among the recomendations were to apply a stronger graphical approach to the pages and articles, utilizing a simple layout for appeal and accessibility; to populate articles with combined student/professor ratings or recommends; and to consider making Page 2 the home page.

I decided to incorporate all of this advice as I felt it was useful.
